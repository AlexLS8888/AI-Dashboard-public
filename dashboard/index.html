AIXI — AI Investment Composite Index · Methodology v1.0
Design Philosophy
AIXI tracks 10 variables across 4 pillars to produce a quarterly tracker of the structural health of the AI investment cycle. Each variable is chosen because it measures a distinct dimension at a different update frequency and from an independent source. The framework is a health tracker, not a prediction engine. The delta (QoQ change in score) is always displayed alongside the absolute level.
Regime Classification
RegimeConditionImplicationScore Range
EXPANSIONAll 4 pillars score ≥ 6. No pillar in active deterioration.Thesis intact. AI cycle structurally healthy. Capital committed, demand real, capability advancing.7.0 – 10.0
WATCHOne pillar deteriorating (score 4–6) while others hold. Divergence signal.Monitor closely. Rotation signal: consider moving from infrastructure toward software/application layer.5.0 – 6.9
TRANSITIONTwo or more pillars deteriorating simultaneously. Structural inflection.Cycle peak likely past. Reduce training infra exposure. Watch for new expansion phase triggers.0.0 – 4.9
Scoring Principles
(1) Each variable scores 0–10 against defined thresholds calibrated on historical data.
(2) Each pillar score = simple average of its variable scores (equal weight unless stated).
(3) Composite AIXI = simple average of the 4 pillar scores (25% each).
(4) The QoQ delta of the composite is displayed alongside the absolute score. Direction matters more than level.
(5) When a variable is unavailable, the pillar uses remaining variables only (no imputation).

Key Structural Limitations
These limitations are structural and cannot be resolved without access to non-public data:

No Remaining Performance Obligations (RPO) — Azure RPO ($315B+) and Oracle RPO ($455B+) represent committed future spend and are 12–24 month leading indicators of CapEx — arguably the strongest leading signal not in AIXI v1.0.
No GPU Delivery Lead Times — NVIDIA B200/GB200 delivery backlogs are the most direct signal of frontier demand. Exists at SemiAnalysis and channel checks but not publicly available systematically.
No Power Queue Data — In 2025–2026, the binding constraint on datacenter expansion is electrical power, not GPU supply. Grid interconnection queue lengths are available but require significant processing.
Limited Enterprise Monetisation — Azure AI, AWS AI, and Google Cloud AI Revenue — the majority of commercial AI spend — are not separately disclosed. Only proxy metrics are available.
